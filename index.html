Proven Leadership, Colorado Values

Meet Kevin

A kid from Hong Kong's public housing, a father of three, and 35 years of showing up for Douglas County.

You may know me from my work on the Douglas County School Board or South Metro Fire Rescue — or maybe from a simple conversation on your doorstep. But before all that, I was just a kid from Hong Kong with a big heart and a thick accent.

My parents couldn't read or write, but they raised me to value hard work and integrity. When I arrived in America, I didn't speak much English — but I spoke the language of perseverance. I've been speaking it ever since.

In 2018, we passed a $250 million school bond and mill levy override that brought new schools, better tools, and renewed trust.

Later, I joined the South Metro Fire Rescue board — where I helped oversee a $200 million budget to support over 570,000 residents. I've never worn the uniform, but I've always had their back.

At a school board forum, someone said I didn't belong — because of how I speak. That moment hurt. But I didn't shout. I didn't quit. I filed a report, took a breath — and kept serving. Because leadership isn't about how you sound. It's about how you serve.

Last year, when a respected Japanese American physician was wrongly accused of bringing communism into a local campaign, I stood up. We gathered 1,200 signatures, with mayors, senators, and neighbors standing shoulder to shoulder. Because injustice doesn't scare me — silence does.
